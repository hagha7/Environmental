Forest Fire

A forest fire is an uncontrollable fire occuring in nature. Even when the fire fighters are here to stop it, even they cannot withstand the fire. Forest Fires start in two ways. One is by ligtning with spontaneous combustion of dry fuel like sawdust and leaves. The other is our own fault; smoking. Also, the fire can cause carbon dioxide into the air. With this, the carbon dioxide will be released into the atmosphere and this can lead to something worldwidely, but not in a good way. Forest fires can kill animals that are important to our ecosystem.

Our Ocean

We are all oblivious to the fact that we take advantage of the ocean. How? The ocean's plants underwater produce half of the world's oxygen. Without the ocean, the clouds wouldn't be able to create fresh water. Also, other than seafood, the algae and kelp are ingredients in peanut butter, soymilk, and others. Another major factor is that the ocean ingredients are also used in medicine to cure cancer, Alzheimer's disease and more. We are starting to lose our ocean because of pollution, careless oil spills.

How To Help

For forest fires, we can stop the smoking habits. One tree can make millions of matches, yet one match can destroy millions of trees. For the ocean, we should be considerate and don't pollute, litter, or spill oil. A drop of water is worth more than a a sack of gold. For animal endangerment, we can stop the poaching. As for electromagnetic pollution, we cannot completely stop it. We can at least stop using our phones and play outside. Good luck and may the forest be with you!!
